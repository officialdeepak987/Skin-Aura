Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

A hair transplant is a surgical procedure that involves moving hair follicles from a "donor site" (usually the back or sides of the head) to a "recipient site" experiencing thinning or baldness.

Yes, hair transplants are generally considered permanent because the transplanted follicles are resistant to the hormones that cause hair loss.

A good candidate is someone experiencing permanent hair loss with stable patterns and sufficient donor hair. They should be in good overall health and have realistic expectations. A consultation is essential to determine suitability based on hair type and scalp condition.

Depending on the number of grafts required, the procedure typically takes between 4 to 8 hours.

The procedure is performed under local anesthesia, so you won't feel pain during the surgery. Some mild discomfort or tightness may be felt during recovery.

Most patients see significant hair growth between 6 to 9 months after the procedure, with full results visible after 12 months.
